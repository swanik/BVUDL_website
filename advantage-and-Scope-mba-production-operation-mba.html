What are the Job Responsibilities of Operations Managers – MBA Operation in distance learning?

The job responsibilities of operations managers are too diverse to be categorized in any one particular functional area.

They mainly deal with

  • Production planning and coordination business organization activities. They may work with various departments including production, sales, pricing, or distribution of goods and services.
  • Overseeing daily production and operations and formulating relevant policies
  • Directing the use of materials as well as human resources for production and operations.
  • Evaluating performance reports and identifying areas of improvement such as possible cost reductions and system efficiency improvements
  • Establish and enforce departmental goals, policies, procedures, and guidelines

Overtime, the field of operations management has evolved to include more and more facets that require professional expertise and skills. One such challenge includes the management of operations in today’s technologically advanced business environment. For instance, modern technology allows us to monitor the demand patterns as well as stock levels continuously and adjust prices rapidly. Effective management of this and numerous other related issues has become an important area of concern for operation managers.

Therefore, it is imperative to acquire specialized knowledge in the area if you wish to pursue a relevant career path. MBA operations management is appropriate for individuals who want to seek high level operations management positions in different kinds of business organizations.

What is the Curriculum like for an MBA Production and Operation management degree?

Many schools allow students to choose a specific area that they wish to specialize in. The courses will therefore depend on the student’s respective concentration subject. Some specialty areas include the following:

  • Operations Management for Competitive Advantage
  • Quality Management and Continuous Improvement
  • International Business Operations Management
  • Introduction to Transportation, Logistics, and Supply Chain Management

Some of the courses that you may have to study include:

  • Operations Strategy
  • General Management and Operations Consulting
  • Developing New Products and Services
  • Supply Chain Management
  • System Design and Business Dynamics
  • Integration of Business Functions
  • Computer Integrated Manufacturing

The courses usually vary depending on the university that you enroll in.

As part of the curriculum, some universities may require you to complete an internship program at either a manufacturing organization or a service firm. In addition to providing you with hands-on experience, this opportunity will also enable you to form a valuable professional network.

Why pursue an Distance Learning Part time MBA Production and Operations Management

Since more and more people are turning to online education, many institutes offer distance learning MBA programs. Online degrees offer a number of advantages. With these programs you’ll be able to:

  • Cut down on costs by not having to travel to a campus-based institute to attend classes
  • Study from any location
  • Complete the program at your own pace by taking up accelerated coursework or enrolling in a self-paced program
  • Hold onto a job while you pursue a degree
  • Benefit from an equally rigorous curriculum and course content that is offered with traditional schools
