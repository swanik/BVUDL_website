Medical Profession as an Career Option

  • In India Medical Profession has been regarded as the most respected and rewarded career option amongst young aspirants.
  • Indian Doctors are acknowledged worldwide for their dedicated contribution and services in the filed of medicine.
  • Their is increasing demand of qualified MBBS Doctors in the India, which comprise worlds second largest population after China.

Current Medical Studies Scenario in India

  • MBBS studies has become choice only for those who are either Top Rankers in Entrances Exam or Students From Financially rich Family background.
  • Students reappearing for entrance in the hope of high score and loosing important years of career.
  • Acute shortage of MBBS Medical professionals in the country of 125 Crore Population.
  • Student are left with saturated options like BAMS, BDS or BPT which has limited scalability and remuneration.
  • Large number of Students opt for Engineering and other profession as an compromise resulting to too much competition and less job satisfaction.

Introduction of National Medical Commission Bill 2019

  • To generate more career options in the filed of Medical education, National Medical Commission bill was passed by the parliament in July 2019.
  • This bill was introduced by Govt India in order to bring transparency and uniformality in the filed of medical education.
  • To encourage Indian student to access overseas MBBS studies options and ease licensing process of practicing in Country after completion of their degree course as existing student intake is not meeting growing demand of MBBS doctors.

Pre NMC Bill Era Secnario

  • Students has to go for multiple exams for admissions in MBBS course resulting in to stress & confusion amongst students and their parents.
  • Post 1992 due to changes in education policies student going aboard for MBBS studies need to appear for MCI screening exam which has very poor passing ratio, due to which doctors were not getting licence to practice in India.
  • The earlier process has scope for manipulation and wrong doing in admission process.

NMC Bill 2019 Highlights

  • One Nation One Exam process for MBBS admission through NEET.
  • To obviate malpractices and promote right talent base in the filed of medicine common NEXT exam is introduced for all final year MBBS student both studying in India and abroad for licensing.
  • Separate MCI screening test for oversees MBBS degree holder is scrapped now.
  • For all PG course admission will be given on the basis of outcome of NEXT. Hence no NEET for PG post 2022.
